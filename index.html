Agile Methodology

From rigid plans to adaptive development

Hello everyone, today I'm going to talk about Agile - an approach that has changed the way many teams build products.

What is Agile?

  • Agile is an iterative approach to project management and software development
  • Instead of betting everything on a "big bang" launch, Agile teams deliver work in small, but consumable, increments
Agile focuses on collaboration, flexibility, continuous improvement, and high-quality results.

The Old Way: Waterfall

  • Linear and sequential process
  • One phase must be completed before the next begins
  • Heavy planning upfront
  • Limited flexibility once started
Waterfall has been the standard for decades. All requirements are collected at the beginning, then design, develop, test and release. The problem is that if the requirements change, you have to start over.

The Problem with Waterfall

Waterfall Issues

  • Average 70% features unused (Standish Group)
  • Late discovery of problems
  • Customer sees product only at the end
  • Changes are extremely costly

Agile Solution

  • Deliver value in 2-4 week cycles
  • Continuous customer feedback
  • Embrace changing requirements
  • Early and often testing

Standish Group Report (2020):

  • Waterfall success rate: 29%
  • Agile success rate: 42%

Birth of Agile

  • In 2001, 17 software developers met at a ski resort
  • Including Martin Fowler, Kent Beck
  • They shared frustrations with traditional methods
  • And created the Manifesto in just 4 days
In 2001, 17 software developers got together in Snowbird, Utah. They discussed their struggles with Waterfall and decided to propose a better way

Agile Manifesto Values

"The best architectures, requirements, and designs emerge from self-organizing teams."
- Agile Principle #11
Individuals and interactions over processes and tools
Working software over comprehensive documentation
Customer collaboration over contract negotiation
Responding to change over following a plan
The Agile Manifesto defined four main values — all about flexibility, collaboration, and delivering real value

Key Agile Principles

  1. Deliver working software frequently
  2. Embrace changing requirements
  3. Work closely with customers and teams
  4. Reflect and improve regularly
Agile promotes frequent delivery, welcomes changes at any stage, encourages collaboration, and values continuous improvement

Popular Agile Frameworks

Scrum

  • Sprints (2-4 weeks)
  • Roles: PO, Scrum Master
  • Daily standups

Kanban

  • Visual workflow
  • WIP limits
  • Continuous delivery
Agile is not a single method — it's a mindset. There are several frameworks that help teams implement Agile principles in practice

When to Use Agile

  • High uncertainty projects
  • Rapidly changing environments
  • Need for customer feedback during development
Agile is great when you're building something new, uncertain, or where customer feedback is critical

Conclusion

  • Agile helps teams stay flexible and customer-focused
  • It's not a magic solution — but a powerful mindset shift
  • Adaptation and learning are at the heart of Agile
Agile is about adapting to change and delivering value continuously. It's not just a process, it's a way of thinking
